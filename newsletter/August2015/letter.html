AASA Retreats and Upcoming Key Highlights

President John Hundt

Spring: Our mid-year retreats in Dallas and Seattle were both great successes. I want to thank Rebecca Napier and Dan Ott, our southern and eastern regional reps for planning and hosting the Dallas event on April 16 and 17. Also, Kira Martin and Shaun Hernandez (west and midwest regional reps) were the gracious planners and hosts in Seattle April 30 and May 1.

I am originally from the Dallas area and it was great to be back in town. The event started out with a raucous dinner at El Fenix restaurant which was a favorite of mine 30 years ago and is still going strong.

Following a theme of “Show me the Money,” the series of presentations and discussions on Friday covered the business of surgical research, revenue cycle, recruitment of faculty, and funds flow. Betti Gardner did her best to liven things up during an early afternoon break with some free style dancing. We hope to see more of this in Chicago.

The Seattle retreat focused on the theme of “Breaking Barriers, Building Bridges: Exploring the Latest Innovations, Collaborations, and Partnerships in Academic Surgery” and began with a Thursday evening mixer at Poquito's Mexican restaurant in Seattle's vibrant Capitol Hill neighborhood.

Presentations from these retreats are available on the AASA website.

Three key upcoming events within AASA include our Fall Annual Conference, Board Elections and our Annual Compensation Survey. This year’s conference will be October 4-6 in Chicago. One of the many strengths of our conference is the alignment with the Surgical Chairs during a Sunday afternoon joint session. This year’s topic will focus on funds flow.

We are in the process of board nominations and elections. If you have any questions about board opportunities please contact one of your regional reps, me, or any board members. The AASA Nominating Committee is led this year by Shaun Hernandez. The committee is charged with preparing a slate of candidates for the following positions this year:

  • Vice President
  • Secretary
  • Communications Chair
  • All Regional Representatives (East, Midwest, South, West)

New board members will be announced at our annual conference. Contact Shaun Hernandez to submit nominations or with any questions about the election process.

We will also announce the results from our annual compensation survey at the annual conference. Merritt Hawkins is completing the survey for us again this year. The partnership for the compensation survey supports anonymous data collection as well as strong data analysis and presentation of results, which benefits us as members. Thank you for taking the time to submit your data when Merritt Hawkins sends the survey in early August.

Look forward to seeing you in Chicago!

 

AASA President John Hundt is a Department of Surgery Administrator at Johns Hopkins Medicine.
