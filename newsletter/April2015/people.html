Highlights of Four AASA Members Planning to Attend the Midyear Retreats


Casie Stevens

Casie T. Stevens
Faculty Affairs Administrator, Department of Surgery
University of Kentucky

Have you ever gone to an AASA Regional retreat? When and where?
Yes, I attended the AASA Regional Retreat in Nashville, TN, in April 2014, and had the opportunity to help organize the Regional Retreat in Lexington, KY, in April 2013.

How do they differ from the Annual AASA National Meeting?
The Regional Retreats have a smaller, more intimate setting that really focuses on networking. There are usually opportunities to tour the campus and catch a glimpse of the daily operations of the department. The Annual AASA Meeting brings together all of the AASA regions for the broad spectrum of what academic medical centers are dealing with. The opportunity to meet with the Surgical Chairs and get their expertise, feedback and buy-in on our work is essential at this meeting.

What are you looking forward to in Seattle/Dallas?
The opportunity to be in Dallas for the AASA Regional meeting is very important for me. I’ve made some great contacts and special friends through the AASA that have helped me to grow in my field and to become a better information liaison for the faculty at the University of Kentucky.

What do you hope to get out of the experience of attending this year's midyear retreat?
I hope to bring back some new cost-saving strategies to help bulk up our bottom line. It’s no secret that funding is tight right now, so Dallas – “Show me the money!”

 

Veronica Getz

Veronica A. Getz
Business Administrator, Department of Surgery
Wake Forest Baptist Health

Have you ever gone to an AASA Regional retreat? When and where?
Yes, my first Regional retreat was in Charleston three years ago and I’ve kept coming back for more, attending the Regional retreats in Kentucky and Nashville.

How do they differ from the Annual AASA National Meeting?
The environment is more informal and there is a smaller group than the National meeting, so you get to know each other a little more and there are more opportunities to engage in discussion. And as a newer member to AASA, I find it much easier to participate in dialogue in the more personal setting versus a larger room of 150 or 200 people.

What are you looking forward to in Seattle/Dallas?
I was born and raised a Texan, so when Rebecca announced the Regional retreat was in Dallas, it was a no-brainer that I would be attending. I’m praying that Tex-Mex will be served for lunch. Hint Hint. In all seriousness, I am looking forward to the Dallas retreat focus of managing margin. Like most others, our institution has gone through massive organizational change including a service-line transformation, which came right on the heels of surviving Epic. We must keep managing margin top of mind, not just decreasing expense, in order to sustain and grow successful services and departments. Our patients depend on it, and I look forward to continue learning how to help us get there.

What do you hope to get out of the experience of attending this year's midyear retreat?
I am looking forward to thinking proactively and leaving Dallas re-energized. We are in the middle of what will no doubt be a challenging budget season, so I am hoping the retreat will provide me the space to think and grow and take a step back from the day-to-day fires, especially those involving cutting costs.

 

Adrienne Mitchell

Adrienne B. Mitchell, MBA, SPHR
Director of HR and Academic Affairs, Department of Surgery
University of Chicago Medicine & Biological Sciences Division

Have you ever gone to an AASA Regional retreat? When and where?
Yes. I attended my first regional retreat last spring (2014) in Denver, CO.

How do they differ from the Annual AASA National Meeting?
The regional retreats are a fantastic opportunity to establish long-lasting professional relationships in a meaningful way. The smaller audience provides the opportunity for everyone to participate in the session discussions and the “after-session” information sharing was just as valuable.

What are you looking forward to in Seattle/Dallas?
Reconnecting with old friends and making new ones.

What do you hope to get out of the experience of attending this year's midyear retreat?
I hope to gain insight into current trends in academic surgical administration, obtain information to advance my area of expertise and enjoy my first visit to Seattle!

 

Elspeth Rogers

Elspeth Rogers
Division Manager, Plastic & Reconstructive Surgery/Bariatric Surgery
Oregon Health and Science University

Have you ever gone to an AASA Regional retreat? When and where?
Yes, I have been to many; most recently was in Wisconsin.

How do they differ from the Annual AASA National Meeting?
The retreats provide an intimate setting and opportunity to really connect with other members. If possible, I enjoy meeting folks before the retreat starts in their work settings to ask specific practice questions.

What are you looking forward to in Seattle/Dallas?
I have not attended the retreat or national meetings in a couple of years due to the timing of a move and a new baby. I am looking forward to reconnecting with my peers.

What do you hope to get out of the experience of attending this year's midyear retreat?
I always look forward to hearing others’ perspectives (we all have the same challenges) and trying out new ways of doing things.
