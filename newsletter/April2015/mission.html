Content to expect at the AASA Midyear Retreats

Summary of Dallas Retreat

Join us for warm, southern hospitality in Dallas on April 16-17, 2015, for our Midyear Retreat organized by the East and South regions.

Our theme this year is “Show me the Money!” It offers a Texas-style agenda exploring the revenue cycle best practices and cost-saving initiatives to fatten the bottom line. This session is hosted at UT Southwestern Medical Center in Dallas.

Our meeting begins Thursday at 7 pm for a welcoming dinner at Gloria’s Latin Cuisine. Friday’s educational session will begin held on the campus of UT Southwestern at 8:30 am and should conclude about 4 pm.

Our exciting and dynamic programs and speakers include:

The Business of Surgical Research: Reporting, Benchmarks, and Incentives
Steven E. Wolf, MD

Dr. Wolf is an established researcher and internationally renowned clinician in the field of burn and trauma care. His experience in burns, critical care and research is comprehensive, which he has used over the last 21 years to become a world-leading researcher and clinician. He is currently serving as the Vice Chair for Research at the University of Texas – Southwestern Medical Center, where he is responsible for stimulating, supporting and managing research activities for the Department.

In his career, Dr. Wolf has 322 published research articles of which 228 were peer-reviewed in leading journals such as the New England Journal of Medicine, Lancet, and Annals of Surgery. In the last 12 months, he was an author in 14 accepted manuscripts in such journals as the American Journal of Physiology, Critical Care and the Journal of Trauma and Acute Care Surgery. He continues to produce annually a “Year in Review” for burn research, and this is widely accepted as the authoritative monitor of the field.

Dr Wolf is also active in gathering research funding. He received an NIH R-01 grant investigating the effects of insulin treatment in the severely burned as Principal Investigator, and has served as the Principal Investigator on eight other major grants from the Shriners’ Hospitals for Children and the United States Department of Defense. He currently holds four more major grants from the Department of Defense totaling $9 million and is the Principal Investigator on an NIH T-32.

Finally, Dr. Wolf is a national and international leader in his field. He is a member of 23 medical societies, serving on governance committees in nine. He currently serves as the Editor-in-Chief for Burns, the leading journal in its field, and sits on the Editorial Board for The Journal of Burn Care and Rehabilitation and Burns and Trauma. He has also been an invited reviewer in 28 other journals, including the Lancet, Annals of Surgery, Pediatrics, American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine and Critical Care Medicine. He has been invited to speak around the world in Norway, Turkey, Singapore, Mexico, Vietnam, China, Brazil, India, Thailand, Australia and New Zealand to discuss his current work. He has served on several Veterans’ Administration Merit Award Study Sections, and is a member of the Shriners’ Hospitals for Children Research Advisory Board. He is currently serving as the Program Committee Chair for the American Burn Association, and is slated to become President of this organization in 2018.

Back to Basics: How Morehouse Healthcare Revenue Cycle leveraged People, Processes, and Technology to move towards Operational Excellence
Eboni L. Martez, MBA, MSW

Eboni L. Martez is Clinical Department Administrator for the Department of Surgery at Morehouse School of Medicine in Atlanta, Ga. She has been with Morehouse School of Medicine since November 2002 and transitioned to the Department of Surgery in July 2012. She has her Master of Business Administration from Kennesaw State University and a Master of Social Work with a concentration in Community Partnerships from Georgia State University. Mrs. Martez obtained her undergraduate degree from the University of Pittsburgh. She also holds a Black Belt in Lean Six Sigma from Georgia State University, College of Business, and is currently a candidate eligible for Registered Health Information Technology credential with American Health Information Management Association.

The High Cost of Recruiting and Reinventing the Traditional Recruitment Process
Brent Dunnigan, Senior Consultant

Currently, Brent Dunnigan is a Senior Consultant in the Department of Academics for Merritt Hawkins, a premier permanent physician search and consulting firm. In addition to his service at Merritt Hawkins, he also has over 15 years of health care experience, including nine years managing biological and blood derivative sales for the southeastern region for AmerisourceBergen Specialty Group prior to his service with Merritt Hawkins. His territory currently includes Texas, Louisiana, Arkansas, Mississippi, Missouri, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Kansas and Nebraska. Brent was Marketer of the Year for Merritt Hawkins in 2014.

Brent will be joined by his colleague, Michael Boes, Marketing Consultant in the Department of Academics for Merritt Hawkins.

We will also be featuring an interactive discussion sessions focusing on cost-saving initiatives, revenue enhancements or strategies that improved the financial position of your department. Additional information regarding this session will be sent in advance of the meeting.

To register, please email Rebecca Napier. To learn more, visit http://aasa1.org/dallas-midyear-retreat.

We hope to see you there!

Summary of Seattle Retreat

Join us in eclectic, trendy and beautiful Seattle on April 30 and May 1, 2015, for our Midyear Retreat hosted by the West and Midwest regions.

Our theme this year is "Breaking Barriers, Building Bridges" and will explore the exciting and innovative approaches our members and other health care leaders are taking to create sustainable partnerships that will help lead to programmatic, operational and financial success.

Our keynote speakers include:

  • Johnese Spisso, RN, MPA, Chief Health System Officer, UW Medicine, and Vice President for Medical Affairs, University of Washington
  • Benjamin Ware Starnes, MD, Professor of Surgery and Chief, Division of Vascular Surgery, University of Washington
  • Erik Van Eaton, MD, Assistant Professor of Surgery and co-founder, Applied Clinical Informatics Research Laboratory, University of Washington

We will also be featuring several "hot topic" interactive discussion sessions focusing on interdepartmental collaborations, the administrator's role in setting new expectations in today's health care environment and innovations in research funding. These will be moderated by Matt Johnson, Senior Manager at ECG Management Consultants.

We hope to see you there!
